# Lean Leadership: 3 Key Elements of Toyota's Learning Organization

0:00 Toyota is renowned for its excellence in manufacturing and continuous improvement.
0:11 But what's the secret behind their success?
0:14 According to Jeffrey Leiker's book The Toyota Way, it all comes down to being a true learning organization.
0:23 The first key element is identifying root causes and developing countermeasures.
0:29 Instead of just firefighting problems, Toyota uses a simple technique called the 5 Whys.
0:35 They keep asking why until they uncover the true root cause, and then implement countermeasures to prevent the problem from recurring.
0:45 The second element is the concept of Hansei. This is all about responsibility, self-reflection, and organizational learning.
0:55 Toyota actually holds Hansei events to reflect on mistakes and weaknesses, and devise ways to improve. As these improvements are made, the learnings are fed back into the organization.
1:07 The third key element is Hoshin Kanri or strategy deployment.
1:14 Toyota cascades aggressive goals from the top down, and each level develops concrete, measurable objectives to support those goals.
1:22 This ensures alignment and accountability throughout the organization.
1:27 The remarkable thing is that it took Toyota over a decade to build this learning organization.
1:33 But their relentless pursuit of improvement, rather than chasing the latest management fad, is what sets them apart.
1:41 If you want to unlock your organization's true potential, start by embracing these three key elements of Toyota's learning organization.
