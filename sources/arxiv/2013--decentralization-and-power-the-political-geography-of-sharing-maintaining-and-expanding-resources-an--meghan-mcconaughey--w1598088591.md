# Decentralization and Power: The Political Geography of Sharing, Maintaining, and Expanding Resources and Coercion

## Evidence span

> Why do central governments decentralize power to subnational units? Given that decentralization necessarily involves a reduction in relative power in favor of state or provincial governments, why would central governments allow this change? This paper posits that decentralization, if it increases the efficiency of governance or the center's ability to govern, can actually increase overall central control despite reducing relative control. I first present a simple model illustrating the logic of this theory, where the central government balances its ability to govern with the agency costs that decentralization involves. I then use cross-national time-series regressions to demonstrate that factors explaining levels of decentralization are the same factors that we should expect if central governments were making decisions in accordance with the theory presented here.
