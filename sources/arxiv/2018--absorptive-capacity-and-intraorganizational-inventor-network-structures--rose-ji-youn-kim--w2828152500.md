# Absorptive capacity and intraorganizational inventor network structures

## Evidence span

> A firm’s absorptive capacity is delineated by its abilities to sequentially identify, assimilate, and exploit valuable external knowledge. Although numerous inquiries have explored how firms identify and exploit valuable knowledge, relatively little work has explored the assimilation dimension of absorptive capacity, let alone how knowledge assimilation and mechanisms used to identify external knowledge are part of an integrated process. We explore how a firm's internal inventor network structure influences its ability to assimilate and exploit external technology. Specifically, we examine how densely connected inventor network structures influence the absorptive capacity of firms in opposite ways.
