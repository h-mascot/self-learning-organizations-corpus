# "The Absorptive Capacity Spectrum: an Investment, a Capability, and a Process? Which Matters Most?"

## Evidence span

> The tremendous growth of the literature on absorptive capacity (AC) has given rise to diverse conceptual views and empirical measures to examine the construct. The literature is also fragmented in examining the role of AC on firm-level outcomes. We identify key conceptual underpinnings behind the diverse conceptualization of AC and their corresponding measures, and discern some underlying patterns: AC is viewed as either an investment for knowledge creation, a capability, or routines and processes for learning. We also categorize commonly studied firm-level outcomes of AC as knowledge enhancement, innovation, and financial performance. Our findings based on meta-analytical techniques show that AC as routines and processes has the highest explanatory power, followed by AC as a capability, and then as an investment.
