# What is Systems Thinking?

0:00 Let's do a little myth-busting about systems thinking.
0:04 What systems thinking is not is just about being organized.
0:10 Can I add A plus B plus C plus D?
0:13 It's definitely a piece of systems thinking, but it goes much further.
0:18 Systems thinking is not thinking systematically.
0:22 So if I were to think systematically through 10 different subsystems of a vehicle,
0:27 and for each of those subsystems, I would ask the question of
0:31 what is the noise contribution to the overall vehicle in this subsystem?
0:36 And I would sort of add the noise for those different subsystems up.
0:39 I would certainly be thinking systematically through the vehicle.
0:42 I'd be going through an enumerated or complete list of all the subsystems.
0:46 But what's fundamentally missing is I'm not thinking about the vehicle as a system.
0:51 I'm not thinking about it as a whole and having some behavior or properties or functionality
0:57 that must be treated holistically at the top level
1:00 rather than at the decomposed level.
1:03 Let me define a system as a set of pieces
1:05 that have relationships, which performs a function.
1:10 So I could talk about three bricks sitting on a table.
1:14 Are these a system?
1:15 Well do they have some relationship between them Not necessarily I could talk about three subsystems of an aircraft the wing the fuselage and the engines Do they have some shared function Yes flying people from A to B
1:28 What I need to add to my definition of systems is that the pieces and their relationships
1:33 work to produce some functionality or performance that is greater than the parts individually.
1:40 And I'm going to call that emergence.
1:42 What does this mean in terms of systems thinking?
1:44 it means that I'm going to take a series of blocks and I'm going to analyze, well, what are all the
1:49 blocks that are in the system? What are all their relationships? And how do we use the knowledge of
1:54 the blocks and their relationships in order to understand what kind of emergence I'm likely to
2:00 see out of it? It's kind of like playing a chess game. You really have to think 10 moves ahead.
2:07 It starts with what is this invention going to be used for? What's its purpose? And then it moves
2:14 into how are we going to assemble the pieces and parts to make it work? And then eventually,
2:20 how will we build it? How will we be able to make it work continuously? It's really
2:27 seeing the whole chessboard. During that creative, inventive process, that's systems thinking.
