# The CEO Must Be the Chief AI Officer

## Evidence spans

> I think the CEO needs to be the chief AI officer. It's not a engineering team thing. It's not like a product team thing.

> good AI products are agentic loops with tools

> after you record a traffic of an agent operating for a day, you can build a pretty good policy

## Claims and limits

- Pedro presents AI adoption as an executive operating-model responsibility rather than a delegated tooling program.
- He describes Brex's production control mechanism as a network-boundary proxy that records agent traffic and derives policies; this is first-person company evidence, not an independent audit.
- The transcript says 98% of requests for the recruiting agent “Jim” pass automatically and 2% use an LLM decision; the denominator and evaluation method are not supplied in the inspected span.
