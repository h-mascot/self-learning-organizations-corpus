# Description of Organizational Learning Network and Identification of Factors Influencing Its Formation Using Network Analysis Method

## Relevance decision

This is direct, named-organization mechanism evidence. The study examines Khorasan Regional Electric Company's organizational learning network using relational data from 327 employees, including experts and managers. It analyzes how knowledge flows through employee relationships rather than merely measuring a generic organizational-learning construct.

## Preserved abstract evidence

The abstract reports that the company's learning network was fragmented and lacked cohesion, with many employees operating in relative isolation and peripheral actors primarily consuming rather than contributing knowledge. Similarity in hierarchical status, gender, education, and work experience influenced connection formation and knowledge-sharing activity. Traditional formal training did not significantly affect formation of the learning network; informal mechanisms such as social interaction and collaborative work were more important. The paper recommends interdepartmental communication, communities of practice, cross-functional working groups, and designated learning facilitators to bridge network gaps and support knowledge exchange.
