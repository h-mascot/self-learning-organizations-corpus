# The AI Native Company

## Verified source excerpt

Most companies are adopting AI tools. AI native companies are redesigning reality.

Let’s be clear about what’s happening right now. Your engineering team is using Cursor or Copilot. PRs are up 3x. Your product team is using Claude to write specs. Your sales team is using AI to draft emails. Everyone’s productivity looks great on paper.

More code getting shipped, but more bugs in production. More features released, but customers aren’t happier. More meetings about “alignment,” but less actual alignment. The tools are working. The system is breaking.

If you’re a CTO or technical founder reading this, you already know something’s off. You can feel it. The AI adoption curve looks perfect. The velocity metrics are up. But the company doesn’t _feel_ faster. It feels louder.

You’re treating AI like a productivity multiplier. It’s not. It’s a _systems redesign opportunity._

This is the difference between companies that use AI and companies that are AI native.

**This will take 15 minutes to read. It’s comprehensive. Bookmark it if you need to come back.**

They buy Copilot licenses. They set up Claude for customer support. They use ChatGPT for brainstorming. They integrate AI into existing workflows. And they call themselves “AI-first” or “AI-powered” or whatever marketing decided sounds good.
