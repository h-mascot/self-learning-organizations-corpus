# Corpus Expansion Plan

## Mission
Build a provenance-first, continuously expanding corpus of self-learning and self-improving organizations: transcripts, primary research, operating patterns, case studies, failures, and evaluations.

## Canonical loop
1. Discover sources across video, podcasts, papers, books, company artifacts, and criticism.
2. Preserve source metadata and rights status before transformation.
3. Extract verbatim text with timestamps/page anchors.
4. Normalize entities, concepts, mechanisms, evidence strength, and claims.
5. Deduplicate at source, transcript, passage, and semantic-claim levels.
6. Derive bounded domain models—not one indiscriminate company brain.
7. Evaluate retrieval, provenance, contradiction handling, freshness, and operational usefulness.
8. Feed failures into new searches, extraction repairs, taxonomy updates, and evaluation cases.

## 20 research lanes
1 taxonomy; 2 YC/Pedro; 3 AI-native companies; 4 learning organizations; 5 autonomous enterprises; 6 cases; 7 failures; 8 academic foundations; 9 organizational learning; 10 cybernetics; 11 lean; 12 RL organizations; 13 AI agents; 14 feedback loops; 15 autonomous-company visions; 16 recursive improvement; 17 organizational memory; 18 experimentation; 19 governance/safety; 20 corpus design/licensing/evaluation.

## Phases
### Phase 1 — Seed and schema
- Ingest six supplied URLs; quarantine irrelevant/mistaken sources.
- Establish source, artifact, segment, claim, entity, mechanism, evaluation, and rights records.

### Phase 2 — Broad discovery
- Search every lane using multiple query families and citation chasing.
- Prioritize primary and transcript-bearing sources.
- Maintain rejected-source log to avoid rediscovery loops.

### Phase 3 — Extraction
- Prefer publisher transcripts/manual captions; fallback to auto-captions, then ASR.
- Preserve raw transcript separately from cleaned text and annotations.
- Store extraction method, language, timestamps, hashes, and confidence.

### Phase 4 — Knowledge layer
- Tag sensor, policy, tool, quality gate, learning mechanism, artifact, human boundary, and measurable outcome.
- Extract claims with supporting/contradicting passages.
- Build company and concept timelines.

### Phase 5 — Evaluation
- Gold questions with exact evidence spans.
- Retrieval recall/precision; citation correctness; freshness; contradiction surfacing; domain-boundary compliance.
- Human review for high-impact claims and commercial redistribution.

### Phase 6 — Continuous improvement
- Failed queries generate missing-source, taxonomy, extraction, or indexing tasks.
- Monthly corpus release with source counts, changes, evaluation scores, and rights exceptions.

## Pedro/YC operating doctrine
- Capture broad raw context, then curate aggressively.
- Organize knowledge around bounded domains and operational objects.
- Compose company intelligence from specialized models/agents.
- Preserve data, business context, and skills; regenerate ephemeral software.
- Every loop needs sensors, policy, tools, quality gates, and learning mechanism.

## Quality gates
- No source without provenance.
- No transformed text replacing raw evidence.
- No material claim without an evidence span.
- No public redistribution assumption when rights are unknown.
- No source counted as relevant before topic validation.
