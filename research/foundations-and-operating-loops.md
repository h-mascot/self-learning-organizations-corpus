## Corpus inventory: seven lanes for self-learning and self-improving companies

**Selection principle:** durable sources with reusable models, evidence, or operating mechanisms. Primary research and first-party cases are prioritized; practitioner books and videos are included when they clarify implementation. Vendor-reported AI-agent outcomes should be treated as leads, not neutral evidence.

# Cross-lane synthesis: a reusable operating model

The strongest sources collectively suggest that a self-improving company needs **seven linked functions**, not merely “more AI”:

1. **Sense:** instrument operations and detect meaningful deviations.
2. **Interpret:** distinguish signal from noise, local symptoms from systemic causes.
3. **Search:** maintain explicit capacity for exploration, not only exploitation.
4. **Experiment:** test interventions with causal or sequential-decision methods.
5. **Act:** deploy changes through bounded authority and clear ownership.
6. **Remember:** encode validated lessons in routines, software, training, and institutional memory.
7. **Reconsider:** use double-loop learning to revise objectives, assumptions, metrics, and governance.

A compact corpus architecture could tag every artifact by:

- **Level:** individual / team / business unit / enterprise / ecosystem
- **Loop:** sensing / interpretation / decision / action / evaluation / memory
- **Learning mode:** exploitation / exploration / single-loop / double-loop
- **Evidence type:** theory / empirical study / field case / first-party claim / benchmark
- **Time horizon:** real-time / operational / quarterly / strategic
- **Control mode:** centralized / distributed / market-like / human-agent hybrid
- **Failure mode:** local optimum / metric gaming / delay / drift / lost knowledge / unsafe autonomy

## Caveats

- **Analogy risk:** RL, evolution, and cybernetics are useful organizational models, but firms are political and social institutions—not unitary agents with stable reward functions.
- **Survivorship bias:** Toyota, Netflix, Booking.com, Microsoft, and similar cases are unusually capable organizations.
- **Vendor bias:** AI-agent customer stories often omit implementation cost, failed pilots, human fallback labor, and baseline selection.
- **Measurement risk:** what is easy to optimize can displace what matters; every learning loop needs guardrails and periodic objective review.
- **Knowledge-retention risk:** more experiments do not produce organizational learning unless results alter routines, incentives, software, training, or strategy.
